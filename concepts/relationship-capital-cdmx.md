# Relationship Capital in CDMX

## The insight
In Mexico City, business relationships are built and propagate within colonias (neighborhoods), not across them. A warm introduction in Polanco chains to another Polanco contact. It does not naturally reach Condesa, Roma, or Lomas Virreyes without a separate relationship bridge.

This means:
- **Saturating one colonia** before expanding to the next is more efficient than spreading thin across the city
- **Two projects in the same colonia simultaneously** burn the same relationship capital — contacts see two asks from the same person
- **A successful referral in one colonia** is not transferable to another without a new relationship bridge

## Why referrals work this way in CDMX
Mexican SME culture is highly relationship-driven. Trust transfers through personal vouching, not cold outreach. A colonia-level social network is tight and overlapping (same gym, same restaurants, same kids' schools). This is the asset — but it has limited bandwidth per person per time window.

## The opportunity
The flip side: once you have strong relationships in one colonia, the penetration rate is very high. A product that dominates Polanco opticals can claim "the Polanco standard" — which is a strong signal for the next colonia expansion.

## Rules
1. **Sequence, don't parallelize** — never run GTM for two projects in overlapping colonias at the same time
2. **One colonia, one project** — assign each colonia cluster to one project at a time
3. **Reference first** — always open with a referral from a trusted contact, never cold outreach
4. **Saturation target** — 70%+ of target businesses in one colonia before expanding
