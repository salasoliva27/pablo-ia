# CDMX Neighborhood Targeting

## The insight
"Mexico City" is too broad. "Mexico" is useless. The right unit is the **colonia** or neighborhood cluster. Different colonias have different socioeconomic profiles, different business densities, and different relationship capital access.

## Why it matters
Mexican SMEs and local service businesses are relationship-driven. A referral from someone in Polanco carries to another Polanco contact. Starting in one tight geography and saturating it beats spreading thin across the city.

## Neighborhood profiles relevant to portfolio

| Colonia | Profile | Projects |
|---|---|---|
| Bosques de las Lomas | High-income residential, gated community culture, organized HOA equivalent | [[wiki/espacio-bosques]] |
| Polanco | Dense SME cluster, optical stores, restaurants, high-end retail | [[wiki/lool-ai]] |
| Lomas Virreyes | High-income residential, health-conscious professionals 35–60 | [[wiki/longevite]] |
| Roma / Condesa | Creative class, independent businesses, tech-adjacent | [[wiki/lool-ai]] |

## Validated rules
- Spanish-first onboarding always, regardless of underlying tech
- MXN pricing always — USD pricing signals "this is for foreigners"
- Relationship capital from one project can spill to another in same colonia
- ⚠️ Two projects in same colonia at same time creates relationship conflict — sequence them
